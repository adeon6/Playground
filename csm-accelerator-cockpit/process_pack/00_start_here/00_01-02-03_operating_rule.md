# 01 -> 02 -> 03 Operating Rule

Use this rule whenever a customer discovery conversation needs to become a buildable SOP.

## The Flow

### 01. Customer discovery conversation
Purpose:
- capture what the client actually said

What belongs here:
- consultant-client dialogue
- pain points
- desired outcomes
- business questions
- source-system clues
- trust criteria
- constraints

What not to do:
- do not clean it into polished requirements
- do not invent missing details
- do not jump straight to workflow design

Shortcut:
- `01 = raw evidence`

### 02. Guided SOP capture
Purpose:
- turn the raw conversation into structured requirements notes

What belongs here:
- business problem
- desired outcome
- scope
- source expectations
- business rules
- outputs
- validation
- risks and gaps

Required discipline:
- mark each point as either confirmed, assumed, or unknown / to discover
- keep the client language where it matters
- surface ambiguity instead of smoothing it over

Shortcut:
- `02 = organized meaning`

### 03. Accelerator SOP
Purpose:
- produce the clean, buildable statement of work for the first slice

What belongs here:
- workflow objective
- scope boundary
- expected sources
- analytical grain
- core logic
- outputs
- validation standard
- known gaps

What not to do:
- do not pretend unknown items are settled
- do not include every conversation detail
- do not let the SOP become a meeting transcript

Shortcut:
- `03 = buildable spec`

## Working Rule

Always go in order:
1. capture the conversation in `01`
2. extract and structure it in `02`
3. tighten it into a buildable SOP in `03`

Never skip `02`.

If you jump from `01` to `03`, you usually miss:
- hidden assumptions
- unresolved source questions
- weak KPI definitions
- unclear scope boundaries
- missing validation criteria

## Decision Test

Before moving from `01` to `02`, ask:
- what did the client explicitly say?
- what sounds important but still ambiguous?
- what would block a build if left unclear?

Before moving from `02` to `03`, ask:
- is this point confirmed, assumed, or unknown?
- does this requirement support a real first slice?
- is the SOP specific enough to build but honest about gaps?

## One-Line Memory Aid

- `01` tells us what happened in the conversation
- `02` tells us what the conversation means
- `03` tells us what we can build now
